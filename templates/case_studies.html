From a Weekend SLA Breakdown to a Repeatable Queue-Diagnostics System

Specific metrics, hours, and financial figures have been generalized or obfuscated. Detailed KPI outcomes are available for private discussion.

Overview

A national teleradiology provider experienced repeated Turnaround Time (TAT) SLA breaches concentrated on evenings and weekends. Operations attributed the failures to unpredictable emergency department demand. I validated the assumption, identified the true failure mode, and delivered an intervention strategy grounded in measurable queue dynamics.

What I Built (Deliverables)

  • Data foundation: Automated ingestion and modeling of HL7 arrivals and operational timestamps in Google BigQuery
  • Scheduling integration: Normalized shift blocks and staffing coverage in Supabase for alignment with production outcomes
  • Queue health KPIs: Purpose-built metrics to distinguish "busy but controlled" from "actively failing"
  • Diagnostic matrix: Python-based classifier segmenting SLA failures into actionable root-cause categories
  • Leadership output: Hour-by-hour operational playbook for targeted staffing and incentives

The Problem

Chronic SLA breaches were treated as operational noise due to limited explicit penalties, but they materially affected renewal leverage and client retention over time.

The Approach

  1. Incident analysis: A weekend breach was initially blamed on a volume spike. Data showed arrivals were normal; capacity dropped.
  2. Expand scope: Months of history showed predictable coverage decay in specific transition windows, amplified by dependence on variable per-case labor.
  3. Remove volume bias: Standard metrics were misleading. I built normalized KPIs:
    • - Garbage Collection Ratio (GCR): measures backlog-recovery mode by tracking the age profile of work being completed
    • - Volatility Score (Coefficient of Variation): measures whether arrivals are truly chaotic or stable
  4. Diagnostic matrix: Combined GCR + volatility + net velocity (clears - arrivals) to classify hours into demand-driven volatility vs inherited backlog.
  5. Make it actionable: Reconstructed hourly roster coverage and paired it with net velocity to show the causal chain (coverage drops -> net velocity negative -> backlog accumulates -> SLA breaches later).

Impact

Prevented misallocation (staffing downstream breach hours) by showing upstream cause. Enabled upstream stabilization via retention/coverage focus and targeted variable incentives ("surge pay") only where volatility justified it.
